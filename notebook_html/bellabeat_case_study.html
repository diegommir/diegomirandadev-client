Case Study - Bellabeat¶

Business Case¶

The objective is to focus on one product and identify growth opportunities for the company, by analysing trends and patterns within usage data of non-company wearable devices.

Bellabeat Producs¶

  • Bellabeat app: The Bellabeat app provides users with health data related to their activity, sleep, stress, menstrual cycle, and mindfulness habits. This data can help users better understand their current habits and make healthy decisions. The Bellabeat app connects to their line of smart wellness products.

  • Leaf: Bellabeat's classic wellness tracker can be worn as a bracelet, necklace, or clip. The Leaf tracker connects to the Bellabeat app to track activity, sleep, and stress.

  • Time: This wellness watch combines the timeless look of a classic timepiece with smart technology to track user activity, sleep, and stress. The Time watch connects to the Bellabeat app to provide you with insights into your daily wellness.

  • Spring: This is a water bottle that tracks daily water intake using smart technology to ensure that you are appropriately hydrated throughout the day. The Spring bottle connects to the Bellabeat app to track your hydration levels.

  • Bellabeat membership: Bellabeat also offers a subscription-based membership program for users. Membership gives users 24/7 access to fully personalized guidance on nutrition, activity, sleep, health and beauty, and mindfulness based on their lifestyle and goals.

Datasets used¶

  • FitBit Fitness Tracker Data

Findings¶

As can be seen in this work, it clearly shows that individuals who exercise regularly are associated with lower BMI/Weight and better sleep patterns and hence better general health. Although we have few data available at this moment, these findings are corroborated by many studies, as we can see at this article published by the Harvard School of Public Health.

We can also see that, on average, the individuals in this sample have shown a preference of to exercise between 5PM and 7PM, with another slightly lower peak of activity between 12PM and 14PM. This is not a surprise once these intervals matches with afterwork and lunch time respectively.

Recommendations¶

With this in mind, my suggested actions are as follows:

  • Make sure these findings are included and up to date within the Bellabeat App product.
  • Include in the Bellabeat Membership product the possibility for the user to schedule alerts for the best time to sleep, as well as if the user is following a good sleep pattern.
  • Suggest the user the best period of the day to exercise, based on his own preferences --- more busy times versus more calm times.
  • Start a viability study to add support for third-party wearable devices, both to Bellabeat App and Bellabeat Membership. This way we can expand our market on these two products without the need to research and develop new hardware.

Verifying FitBit Dataset¶

  • The dataset was downloaded and unzipped.

  • It contains several files with wearables data from 33 unique individuals.

  • The data from dailyCalories_merged, dailyIntensities_merged, and dailySteps_merged files are all merged inside dailyActivity_merged. So they are going to be discarded.

  • hourlyCalories_merged, hourlyIntensities_merged, and hourlySteps_merged all follow the same file structure (same # of observations), so they were merged in one dataframe called hourActivity.

  • minuteCaloriesNarrow_merged, minuteIntensitiesNarrow_merged, minuteMETsNarrow_merged, and minuteStepsNarrow_merged all follow the same file structure (same # of observations), so they were merged in one dataframe called minuteActivity.

  • minuteCaloriesWide_merged, minuteIntensitiesWide_merged, and minuteStepsWide_merged has pivoted data from their "Narrow" counterparts.

  • heartrate_seconds_merged has 5 seconds interval measurements of the heart rate throughout the day. Has data of just 14 unique individuals.

  • sleepDay_merged has daily information about # of sleep, sleep time in minutes and bed time in minutes. Has data of just 24 unique individuals.

  • weightLogInfo_merged weight and BMI along with the date when it was measured. Has data of just 8 unique individuals.

First point to analyse is the correlation between Weight/BMI and other features. Some common-sense correlations were verified as expected. For example, BMI has a high negative correlation to activity time. But one of the BMI correlations drawn my attention.

The data shows a very high correlation between BMI/Weight and Total Sleep Records. Total Sleep Records data shows the number of times an individual went to sleep. It is expected to be 1, as this is the average daily number of times one is expected to go to sleep. A number higher than 1 may indicate an irregular sleep pattern. So, the correlation between these two features is interesting.

As expected, the more intense the exercise, the more calories are burned. Although the correlation between Calories and Steps are high, if the objective is to lose weight, data shows that high intensity exercise are more effective in burning calories.
